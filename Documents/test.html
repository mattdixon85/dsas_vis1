Differences in Crime Between Seattle & San Francisco

Matt Dixon

Saturday, January 23, 2016

Seattle and San Francisco display very different profiles of crime when viewed as a weekly cycle. Seattle crime is at its lowest on Fridays and the weekend, whereas San Francisco shows the opposite effect.

This is in spite of a larger population in Seattle. Wikipedia reports Seattle's population as likely to be 662,000 in the 2015 census, whereas San Francisco is reported to be 852,000 in July 2014. I suggest that Seattle has a significant problem with crime, and am interested in what is driving the extremely high volumes of crime on Mondays and Tuesdays.
